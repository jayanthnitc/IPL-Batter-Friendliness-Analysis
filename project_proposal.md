# Impact of Batter-Friendly Conditions on IPL Scoring Trends (2008–2025)

## Project Objective

To analyze whether IPL cricket has become increasingly batter-friendly over time by studying scoring patterns, boundary frequency, bowling effectiveness, and match outcomes.

---

## Background

In recent IPL seasons, teams regularly score 200+ runs and successfully chase large targets.

Many fans and experts believe this is because:

* Pitches are becoming flatter
* Batters are becoming more aggressive
* Boundaries are shorter
* Teams prioritize attacking cricket

This project aims to determine whether data supports these claims.

---

## Problem Statement

Has IPL become significantly more batter-friendly between 2008 and 2025?

---

## Research Questions

### RQ1

Has the average innings score increased over time?

### RQ2

Has the average run rate increased over time?

### RQ3

Are more sixes being hit than before?

### RQ4

Has bowling become less effective?

### RQ5

Have 200+ scores become more common?

### RQ6

Which venues are the most batting-friendly?

---

## Hypotheses

### H1

Average innings scores have increased over time.

### H2

Average run rates have increased over time.

### H3

Sixes per match have increased over time.

### H4

Bowling economy rates have increased over time.

### H5

The number of 200+ totals has increased significantly.
